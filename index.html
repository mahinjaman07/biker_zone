Frequently Asked Questions

What are the most important things I should know about riding a bike?

Safety First! Always obey the rules of the road. Obey all traffic signals, signs, and laws. Get in the mindset of “driving” your bike—not just “riding” your bike. This will help you be a more focused and legally compliant bike rider.

How can I tell if my helmet is old and I need a new one?

To determine if your helmet is old and needs replacement, inspect it for visible damage like cracks or dents. Check the age, as helmets generally have a lifespan of 5-7 years. Ensure a snug fit and examine the condition of straps and buckles. If the foam inside is compressed, brittle, or shows signs of damage, it may not provide adequate protection. Replace helmets involved in crashes, regardless of visible damage. Additionally, stay informed about current safety standards, and replace your helmet if it no longer meets them. When in doubt, prioritize safety and invest in a new helmet.

My bike has been in storage is it safe to ride?

Before riding a bike that has been in storage, perform a thorough inspection. Check the tires for any signs of dry rot or damage, and ensure they are properly inflated. Examine the brakes to ensure they are functioning well and not worn down. Lubricate the chain and moving parts, and test the gears and shifters. Check for any loose bolts or components. Verify that the lights and reflectors are working if your bike is equipped with them. If the bike has been stored for an extended period, it's advisable to take it for a short test ride in a safe area to ensure everything is in working order before embarking on longer rides. Regular maintenance and checks will contribute to a safer and more enjoyable biking experience.
